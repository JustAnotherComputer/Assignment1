My Interest in IT

Information Technology, or IT, has been called “the future” for a while now. Originally, I had no real interest in IT beyond playing video games and using the computer to browse the internet and read stories or watch something. However, when I started studying accounting, a family member remarked that ‘IT was the future, and one day, even the person who does accounting will be in fact, an IT specialist’. I didn’t exactly agree with this sentiment, of course. However, after giving it some thought I came to the conclusion that IT was prevalent in all fields, and this included accounting. In fact, IT is extremely useful in accounting as it reduces the margin of error and reduces tedious and time-consuming tasks. Time, which is precious resource in this day and age, can be saved by integrating IT into accounting. In addition to this, I realised that I had never really explored what IT truly was, and so I decided to learn what IT was all about.

I enrolled with OUA as part of an accounting degree, then picked Introduction to Information Technology as an elective. For that reason, I enrolled in RMIT.

My expectations of this course are fairly open. I expect to gain knowledge about the various fields IT engages in, hopefully finding something that piques my interest enough to study further. I am somewhat interested in programming, which I am studying as my second subject in this study period. However, I think gaining a broad introduction to IT as a whole will be extremely useful in selecting what field would further my goals and interests.
