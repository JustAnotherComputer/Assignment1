Team Dynamic

Image sourced from https://testyourself.psychtests.com/bin/transfer

A team has many different roles within it. One role which I don’t enjoy, nor aspire to get, is the role of Team Leader. I prefer carrying out my instructions to the best of my ability; more of a ‘following instructions’ type than a ‘leader’ person. However, I don’t shy away from discussing things. If there is one thing that I have learned over the years, it is that proper communication prevents misunderstandings. Good communication makes a team perform properly.

Now, there are a few things to note. I don’t particularly enjoy large gatherings or unnecessary talking; however, I don’t shy away from talking in a group either. In any team, I prefer to be the supportive team member who does their task to the very best of their ability and resolve to keep conflicts to a minimum. If there are new things to learn, I prefer having them explained to me first, before I try them out on my own. I like things being organised and manageable; small to-do lists make the larger task seem less daunting and easier. As a result, I’m always making lists and breaking down tasks into smaller bits. Of course, I don’t actually enjoy imposing my way of working on other people. Everyone has their own way of doing things, and respecting that is also part of what makes a team. However, I also dislike it when team members don’t talk to each other. Open communication and honesty when meeting deadlines are very important in a task. It’s important to keep everyone up to date on how far you’ve come along – and in some cases it may even provide adequate motivation for people to meet their deadlines.

The results of my quizzes all point to these aspects of my personality. Of course, I should add that adapting to new environments is also an essential skill, and one’s role in a team may change accordingly.
